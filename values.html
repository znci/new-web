Our Core Values

At znci, we have 7 core values that we believe are essential to not only us, but to the web as a whole.

1. Privacy

We believe that privacy is a fundamental human right. Privacy is the foundation of freedom, and without privacy, there is no freedom. We believe that privacy is essential to a free and open web.

2. Accessibility

We believe in the 21st century, it's unacceptable that people are still being excluded from the web. Whether it's because of a disability, or because of a slow internet connection, we believe that everyone should be able to access the web.

3. Open Source

We believe that open source is the future of software development. Open source software is more secure, more reliable, and more trustworthy than closed source software. While not all of our services are open-source, we strongly believe open-source is the future.

4. There's no such thing as a stupid question

We believe that there's no such thing as a stupid question. We believe that everyone should be able to ask questions without fear of being judged.

5. Competition drives innovation

We believe that competition is important. We believe that competition drives innovation, and that without competition, the web would be a much worse place. Unfortunately, the web is dominated by a few large companies, and we believe that this is a problem.

6. Free Speech is a fundamental human right

Censorship kills creativity and innovation. We believe that free speech is why the web is as developed as it is today. We believe that free speech is a fundamental human right, and that everyone should be able to express their opinions without fear of being censored or loosing opportunities.

7. You can build a better web.

The entire purpose of znci is to build the web we want to see. In these 7 core values, we've outlined what we believe is important to the web. We believe that you can build a better web.

If you believe in these 7 core values, we encourage you to join us in building a better web. Shoot us an email at hello@znci.dev.

The web is slowly becoming a worse place. Our mission is to create the web we want to see. We strongly recommend you read the Electronic Frontier Foundation's issues page to learn more about the issues we're facing today.
